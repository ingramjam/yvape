PRIMARY PROGRAM

School gives student the option of choosing YVAPE instead of or in addition to other programs

OVERFLOW PROGRAM

School refers student to YVAPE when the school's primary program is full

YVAPE AT A GLANCE

COACHING CALLS • EDUCATIONAL VIDEOS • DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

Students complete two phone sessions with a YVAPE coach, watch at least three YVAPE videos, and engage in 4-5 discussion questions with their parent/guardian, school, and/or YVAPE coach. Students can also request an optional follow-up call for additional support. The program can be completed in 1 day to 4 weeks, depending on the student's schedule.

YVAPE Coaching Calls

focus on building rapport with students, helping them to think about why they use nicotine and/or marijuana and how to make healthier choices.

YVAPE Videos

educate students on the mental and physical effects of nicotine and marijuana use, how to deal with stress, how to take personal responsibility, and how to ask for help.

HOW YVAPE WORKS

SCHOOL ENROLLS STUDENT IN YVAPE

1

The student is found with, under the influence of, or selling nicotine and/or marijuana at school. The school recommends the student complete YVAPE. The student must be at least 12 years old and have access to a phone and email.

2

The school gets verbal consent from the student and parent/guardian (of students 12-17 years old) to enroll the student in YVAPE. School then enrolls youth via the opt out form.

3

School personnel visits YVAPE.ORG and submits contact information for themselves, the student, and parent/guardian. YVAPE enrollment materials are available in English and Spanish.

YVAPE INFORMS THE SCHOOL

PROGRAM COMPLETION

The school and parent/guardian (of students 12-17 years old) will be notified when the student completes YVAPE and the student will receive a certificate of completion.

PROGRAM INCOMPLETE

The school, student, and parent/guardian (of students 12-17 years old) will be notified 4 weeks after enrollment if the student has not completed YVAPE.
